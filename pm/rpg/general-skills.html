General Skills

Core Competencies & Roll Mechanics
The Chartered Realm v0.8.1 — Engine Codex
```

Six skills available to all characters, regardless of Job or Industry. Each skill requires a test (2d6 + relevant stat, plus the listed bonus). General skills represent tactical competencies that anyone can develop through play. The distinction from Job Passives is clear: Passives define your archetype; Skills are tools you choose to use.

1. Exudes Psychological Safety

Exudes Psychological Safety
Your presence creates conditions where NPCs will speak honestly. When engaging an NPC in dialogue, you can invoke this skill to force honest engagement. Make a test: if you succeed (7+), they answer your real question honestly. On failure (6−), they resist openly—you learn they're hiding something, but not what.
Bonus: +2 to the roll.
Trigger: Negotiation or Discovery scene, when an NPC is deflecting or giving scripted answers.
Once per NPC per session.

2. Active Listening

Active Listening
You extract the real concern beneath an NPC's stated position. When an NPC has stated their position or request, make a test. On success (7+), the GM articulates the NPC's underlying concern, fear, or desire. On failure (6−), you misread them—the GM tells you what you think they want (incorrect), and you must act on that false assumption or try again later.
Bonus: +2 to the roll.
Trigger: After an NPC has stated their position.
Once per NPC per session.

3. Decisive Action

Decisive Action
You commit publicly to one course of action and push forward with resolve. When you invoke this skill before making a test, gain +3 to that test. However, failure becomes public and consequential—no quiet backing out. Everyone knows you tried and failed.
Bonus: +3 to one related test. If you fail (6−), the failure is witnessed and shapes the NPC's opinion of you permanently.
Trigger: Before making a test where public commitment matters.
Once per decision point (i.e., once per major choice).

4. De-escalation

De-escalation
You lower the emotional temperature in a scene. When tension is high and an NPC is escalating, make a test to de-escalate. On success (7+), move their disposition one step down the track (Hostile → Skeptical, etc.). On failure (6−), the tension remains; your attempt may have made things worse.
Bonus: +2 to the roll. Cannot be combined with pressure tactics or aggressive rolls in the same beat—you're making a conscious choice to cool things off.
Trigger: When an NPC is escalating or a Crisis scene is about to explode.
Once per NPC per scene.

5. Data-Driven Argument

Data-Driven Argument
You support your position with documented evidence, making it harder to dismiss. When making a persuasion or negotiation roll where you have concrete evidence, gain +3 to the roll. On success, the NPC must address your evidence—they can't pretend it doesn't exist.
Bonus: +3 to the roll when evidence actually supports your position (written records, testimony, physical proof).
Trigger: Any roll where you're citing concrete evidence.
Unlimited use, but only when evidence applies.

6. Delegation

Delegation
You assign an NPC a meaningful task and trust them with it. Make a test with +2 bonus. On success (7+), the NPC completes the task and becomes a reliable asset you can delegate to again this arc. On failure (6−), the task doesn't get done, but you learn something valuable about what blocks them—a fear, conflict, or hidden priority.
Bonus: +2 to the roll.
Trigger: When you want an NPC to act on your behalf (carry a message, investigate, advocate for you).
Once per NPC per arc.
```
