Who I Am

Geologist, project manager, data nerd, and generalist problem-solver based in Anchorage, Alaska. Over a decade of field and office experience in geotechnical investigation, environmental work, and applied research — from permafrost work in the Arctic to asteroid porosity studies presented at GSA. My work sits at the intersection of subsurface uncertainty and infrastructure decisions, and I care a lot about making complex technical information accessible and usable in the field.

When I’m not on a drill site or writing reports, I’m probably running trails, running a tabletop RPG campaign, building something on a 3D printer, or chasing down some obscure dataset that probably doesn’t have a good visualization yet. This site is where the technical tools live — built for engineers and technicians working in Alaska’s challenging environments, and freely available. Everything here is a personal project and a perpetual work-in-progress.
