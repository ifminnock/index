Pi-group scaling laws — Holsapple (1993) / Melosh (1989)

Geotechnical connection: Pi-group scaling is the same dimensional analysis framework applied to dynamic loading in geotechnical engineering—penetration resistance, pile driving energy, and blast-induced ground deformation all follow analogous scaling laws. Impact cratering simply extends this to hypervelocity regimes where material response governs crater morphology the same way soil strength governs bearing capacity under dynamic loads.
